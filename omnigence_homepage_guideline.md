# Omnigence Homepage Guideline for Web Designer

## 1. Goal

This homepage should keep the current visual style and single-page layout, while sharpening the product story.

The page should communicate:

- Omnigence is an AI agent for operations
- It turns business inputs into structured business data
- Human approval stays in the loop
- This workflow unlocks higher-level intelligence such as proposal intelligence
- Operational rigor and trust are part of the product, not an afterthought

This guideline is for design and content direction only. It is not a technical spec.

---

## 2. Core Positioning

### Main positioning
Omnigence is an AI agent you can train to run business operations.

### Product scope
The homepage should reflect a broader platform story, not a single-use-case product.

The homepage should support themes such as:

- proposal intelligence
- invoicing
- bankbook / reconciliation
- document-driven operational workflows

### Important note
Do **not** mention technical implementation terms such as:

- vector database
- embeddings
- retrieval architecture
- semantic similarity engine

These are internal / technical concepts, not homepage language.

---

## 3. Homepage Narrative

The homepage should read in this order:

1. **Hero**
2. **How it works**
3. **What intelligence this unlocks**
4. **Trust and control**
5. **Final CTA**

The page should **not** mix all intelligence capabilities directly into the 3-step workflow.  
Instead:

- the workflow explains how the system works
- the next section explains what that workflow enables

---

## 4. Visual Direction

Keep the current style for now.

### Existing style to preserve
- clean single-page design
- large white / light-gray canvas
- soft blue / indigo gradient accents
- minimal, modern product-marketing look
- rounded product cards
- large whitespace
- alternating two-column sections
- lightweight, calm UI illustrations
- Apple-clean / startup-clean feel
- restrained typography
- simple CTA buttons
- placeholder product visuals are acceptable for this phase

### Tone of the visuals
The page should feel:

- calm
- trustworthy
- modern
- product-led
- operationally serious
- not overly technical
- not overly corporate
- not playful

---

## 5. Audience

The homepage should work for a mixed audience:

- engineering firms
- SMB owners
- sales teams
- estimators
- operations-minded business users

Because the audience is mixed, the copy should stay outcome-oriented and easy to scan.

---

## 6. Hero Section Guidance

## Objective
Introduce Omnigence as an AI agent for operations, not just a document tool.

## Current direction to keep
Use the same hero composition style:

- left: headline, supporting copy, CTAs
- right: product illustration / concept UI

## Hero messaging direction
The headline should stay broad enough to support multiple workflows.

Example direction:
- An AI agent you can train to run your operations
- Turn documents, approvals, and company knowledge into automated workflows

### Hero supporting copy guidance
The supporting line should make clear that Omnigence works with business inputs and turns them into usable operational outcomes.

It should suggest:
- structured business data
- automated workflows
- reusable company knowledge
- human oversight

### CTAs
Keep two CTAs in hero:

#### CTA 1 — for buyers / operators
Examples:
- See How It Works
- Request a Demo

#### CTA 2 — for technical evaluators
Examples:
- Explore API
- View API
- Developer Docs

### Hero illustration guidance
Keep the current card-based / floating UI language.

The hero visual should show a system flow such as:
- business input
- structured business data
- AI task output
- approval / review
- an operational or intelligence result

This should feel like a business system, not a chat toy.

---

## 7. “How It Works” Section

## Objective
Preserve the 3-step workflow.

This section should stay process-oriented.

### The 3 steps must stay:
1. input
2. transform
3. human approval

These should remain the backbone of the homepage.

---

### Step 1: Input
Recommended section direction:

**Connect to your business**

This should replace or evolve beyond “Upload your request.”

Reason:
“Upload your request” feels too narrow and too document-only.  
“Connect to your business” is broader and better fits the product direction.

### What this step should communicate
Omnigence can start from real business inputs such as:

- PDFs
- spreadsheets
- images
- exported files
- documents
- operational records
- plain text instructions

### Visual guidance
Placeholder illustration is fine.
Can show:
- files entering the system
- upload area
- connected data sources
- intake panel

---

### Step 2: Transform
Recommended section direction:

**Turn raw business inputs into structured outputs**

This step should explain that Omnigence converts messy business inputs into clean, usable, structured business data and documents.

### Important messaging
This section should not sound like “simple formatting.”  
It should imply:
- normalization
- structuring
- reuse
- downstream intelligence

### Example meaning
The output should feel like:
- finished documents
- structured business records
- reusable company knowledge
- data that can support future decisions

### Visual guidance
Keep the existing visual pattern:
raw inputs on one side -> refined output on the other side

---

### Step 3: Human Approval
Recommended section direction:

**You approve what goes out**

This step should remain explicit.

### What this step should communicate
Nothing leaves the team without review.

This is important for:
- operational control
- risk reduction
- trust
- auditability

### Visual guidance
Use approval / review / exception-style cards.
The system should feel like a copilot with controls, not a fully autonomous black box.

---

## 8. Section After “How It Works”

## Objective
After the 3-step workflow, show what the workflow unlocks.

This section should answer:

**What intelligence does this operational foundation enable?**

This should be a new section, not forced into the 3-step section.

---

## 9. “What Intelligence This Unlocks” Section

## Strategy
Lead with **proposal intelligence** as the primary flagship example.

Use **bankbook / reconciliation** as the secondary supporting example.

### Why this split works
- Proposal intelligence is strategic and differentiating
- Reconciliation proves rigor, operational control, and risk handling

---

### Primary example: Proposal intelligence
This should be the main intelligence story.

### What it should communicate
Omnigence can help teams:

- reuse past business knowledge
- generate better proposals faster
- recommend the right structure or sections
- support more consistent proposal creation
- surface relevant context instead of starting from scratch

### Copy direction
Use business language such as:
- reuse past work
- generate stronger proposals faster
- start from the right structure
- turn company records into proposal intelligence

Do not use technical language.

### Visual guidance
Proposal intelligence visuals can include placeholder concepts such as:
- project inputs
- recommended proposal structure
- highlighted matching business context
- generated proposal draft
- approval / review panel

---

### Secondary example: Bankbook / reconciliation
Use this as a supporting proof point, not the homepage’s main storyline.

### What it should communicate
The same platform can also power operational workflows that require:
- discrepancy detection
- review
- matching
- auditability
- exception handling

This proves that the system is not only generative but also operationally disciplined.

### Visual guidance
Keep or adapt the current reconciliation-style cards:
- discrepancies flagged
- items matched
- request changes / approve controls
- financial summary or report card

---

## 10. Trust and Control Section

## Objective
Make trust visible as a product feature.

These items should be reflected in the page, either as a dedicated section or clearly woven into the intelligence section.

### Must include these concepts
- human approval
- explainability
- audit trail
- confidence indicators

### Optional trust phrases
- why it matched
- review before approval
- tracked decisions
- confidence shown inline
- auditable outputs
- explainable recommendations

### Design direction
Trust should not feel like a legal disclaimer.
It should feel embedded in the product UX.

Examples:
- confidence badge
- “why this was recommended”
- approval activity log
- audit history timeline
- discrepancy explanation panel

---

## 11. Content Strategy Rules

### Rule 1
Keep the homepage readable for non-technical decision-makers.

### Rule 2
Use behavior-based language, not infrastructure language.

Good:
- finds relevant past work
- recommends likely next steps
- structures messy business inputs
- keeps humans in control

Avoid:
- embeddings
- vector search
- retrieval stack
- semantic indexing

### Rule 3
Do not make quoting the only homepage identity.
Proposal intelligence can lead the examples, but the homepage should still feel like a broader operational AI platform.

### Rule 4
Do not let the site feel like a generic document conversion tool.
The workflow creates structured business memory and usable operational context.

### Rule 5
Keep the copy concise, clean, and product-like.

---

## 12. Recommended Single-Page Structure

Suggested order:

### 1. Hero
- broad AI operations positioning
- two CTAs
- product/system illustration

### 2. How It Works
- Connect to your business
- Transform inputs into structured business data
- Human approval

### 3. What Intelligence This Unlocks
- Proposal intelligence (primary)
- Reconciliation / bankbook workflow (secondary proof)

### 4. Trust and Control
- explainability
- confidence indicators
- audit trail
- approval checkpoints

### 5. Final CTA Banner
Use the existing bold / gradient CTA section style.

### 6. Footer
Keep clean and light.

---

## 13. Copy Tone Guidance

The copy should feel:
- clear
- calm
- operational
- intelligent
- trustworthy
- modern

Avoid sounding:
- overly technical
- academic
- chatbot-like
- gimmicky
- too broad and vague

### Preferred language style
- short sentences
- product language
- minimal jargon
- concrete outcomes
- control-oriented phrasing

---

## 14. Placeholder Illustration Guidance

Since illustrations are placeholders for now, the designer should focus on consistency more than detail accuracy.

### Placeholder visuals should still imply:
- workflow
- structure
- review
- intelligence
- operational rigor

### Acceptable placeholder motifs
- cards
- flow arrows
- check states
- discrepancy states
- finished document previews
- proposal draft previews
- confidence / explanation callouts
- approval buttons
- activity history panels

---

## 15. CTA Guidance

## Hero CTAs
Two CTAs should remain.

### CTA for buyers / operators
- See How It Works
- Request a Demo

### CTA for technical evaluators
- Explore API
- Developer Docs

## Final CTA section
Keep this broad, not quote-only.

Because quoting is just one use case, the bottom CTA should stay aligned to the broader operational platform.

Examples of direction:
- Stay focused on growth. We’ll help you reduce operational risk.
- Move faster without losing control.
- Turn business inputs into reliable operational workflows.

---

## 16. What the Homepage Should Not Do

Do not:
- lead with infrastructure terms
- look like a developer tool homepage
- look like a generic PDF parser
- make proposal intelligence the only product identity
- overload the page with too many examples
- mix workflow steps and capability explanations too tightly
- make the product feel fully autonomous with no controls

---

## 17. Final Creative Summary for Designer

The site should feel like:

**A calm, modern AI operations platform that turns messy business inputs into structured outputs, keeps humans in control, and unlocks higher-level intelligence such as proposal intelligence and reconciliation workflows.**

The current web style is directionally correct and should be preserved.

The main design improvement is not a visual overhaul.  
It is clearer content hierarchy:

- first show how the system works
- then show what intelligence that foundation unlocks
- then reinforce trust and control

---

## 18. One-Line Internal Reminder

For this homepage, the product story is:

**Input -> structured business data -> human approval -> operational intelligence**
